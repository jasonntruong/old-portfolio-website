cov-ID

Awarded Best Hardware Hack Sponsored by Digi-key at yuHacks 2021 (York University)

cov-ID is an NFC card and app duo. There are 2 sides to the app; the user side and service side.

The user side allows everyone with a cov-ID card to see their recent test information (test result, test date, test location) and where they have scanned their cov-ID.

The service side will be the people who scan the cov-ID, i.e restaurants scanning customer's cov-ID. With the app, they can scan and get the previously listed information from their customers or set restrictions on who's allowed in: i.e refusing those who do not take the vaccine, those that haven't been tested in x amount of days, and those who were recently tested positive but refusing to quarantine.

This ultimately makes businesses and public places more safe and also people more comfortable to go outside as they can go to businesses whose restrictions they agree with.

cov-ID was built with NFC technology, Google Cloud's Google Maps API, Java, XML, and Android Studio!

Lucid

Top 6 Finalist at qHacks 2021 (Queen's University)

Lucid aims to make life easier for the blind.

It's an app whose main function is to use the phone's camera and text-to-speech to tell the user what objects are in front of them (object recognition) or what the text in front of them says (text recognition). Ex: Grocery shopping, blind people can't tell if an apple is red or green!

We also have incredible UI features built in like physical/tactile feedback on button clicks, text-to-speech to describe the activity that appears on the screen, voice command startup, speech speed customization, and many other features so that Lucid is perfectly curated for users without sight.

Lucid was built with Google Cloud's Vision API, Machine Learning Kit, Java, XML, and Android Studio!
