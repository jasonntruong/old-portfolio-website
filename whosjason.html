Who I Am

I'm Jason Truong, a Vietnamese second year computer science student at Ryerson University in Toronto, Canada.

My interest in computer science sparked at a young age. In grade 4 I was hooked on coding flash games and objects into Roblox...but with little success.

My obsession would lay dormant until I took a computer science course in grade 11. This would soon rekindle my childhood fascination, and for the first time, I was enjoying school!

Fast forward to today, if all goes well, I'll be graduating with a BSc. Hons degree in Computer Science in 2025 as I'm enrolled in a 5 year coop program.

Experience

Code Sensei at Code Ninjas

At Code Ninjas, my role as a Code Sensei is to teach the students how to code!

Using Java, JavaScript, Lua, and Scratch, I am able to organize 1-3 hour lessons to train their programming logic and show them how to "think like a computer" - rather than the coding syntax.

Since the ninjas are in elementary school, I am also responsible for keeping them engaged, focused, and answering their coding queries in the most simplest way possible.


Online Freelance Tutor

At the start of the school year and prior to becoming a Code Sensei, I decided to start online tutoring since I did not work during the Summer due to covid.

I tutor elementary school students via Zoom. My responsibilities include helping my students with their homework, re-explaining key concepts from class, assigning homework for practice, and answering questions via email off hours.

Ultimately, since starting in September 2020, I have been responsible for the academic success of my students.

Activities

Ryerson's Computer Science Course Union

The Ryerson CSCU is a group of elected students that aim to provide computer science students with the best opportunities and experience.

Currently, as VP Communications, I am the public relations contact for the CSCU. I also design and promote all of our events via our Instagram, Twitter, Facebook, website, by contacting other student socities, and through our monthly newsletter distributed to all CS students at Ryerson!


In my first year, as the Deputy of VP Operations, I perform tasks that range from planning events/workshops to advocating the concerns of CS students to the CS department heads.

The CSCU also collaborates with student groups to further provide students with larger scale workshops. One example was an event where I, on behalf of the CSCU, collaborated with WiCS (Women in CS), LUG (Linux User Group), DSC (Developer Student Club), and Guelph and McMaster University to host "Technical Interview Day"

At this event, we invited 5 ex-FAANG Ryerson, Guelph, and McMaster students to talk about the interview process and the tips and tricks to ace the Technical Interview!
